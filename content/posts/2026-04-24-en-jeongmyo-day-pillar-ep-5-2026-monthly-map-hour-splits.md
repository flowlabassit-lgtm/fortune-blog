<h2>What Jeongmyo Actually Is (30-second read)</h2>
<p>丁 is yin fire — a lamp, not a bonfire. 卯 is pure yin wood — the rabbit, kindling. 卯 sits directly under 丁 as a <strong>印星 (Seal / Resource)</strong> element, which means your chart is wired to <em>absorb attention and convert it into authority</em>. That's a gift in years when the sky is quiet. In 2026, the sky is not quiet.</p>

<p>For a Jeongmyo reader, the 2026 yongsin priority is <strong>Water first, Metal second</strong>. Fire is already loud. Your job is to cool and shape, not add heat.</p>

<h2>Where I Got This Wrong Initially</h2>
<h3>The naive reading of a 丙午 year</h3>
<p>My first instinct — and the advice you'll see on most Korean Saju blogs — was "丁 loves fire company, so 2026 is a lucky year." Wrong. 丁 plus 丙午 is one candle next to a furnace. The candle doesn't get brighter. It disappears.</p>
<p>The correction: in 丙午 years, Jeongmyo thrives by <strong>becoming the thing fire needs (wood)</strong> or <strong>the thing that contains it (metal tools, water boundaries)</strong> — not by matching its energy. I learned this the slow way by tracking reader feedback on Ep. 2-4 and noticing Jeongmyo readers who "leaned in" to visibility burned out by July.</p>

<h2>The 4 Hour Pillar Splits — A Single Named Case</h2>
<p>Meet <strong>Minseo</strong>, a 丁卯 day pillar freelance designer (invented example used consistently below — no real person).</p>

<h3>1. Wood-hour (寅/卯 hour) Jeongmyo → "Double Seal"</h3>
<p>Minseo with a 卯 hour pillar has too much kindling. She feels inspired 24/7 but finishes nothing. 2026 rule: pick <strong>one</strong> project in March, refuse every new idea until September.</p>

<h3>2. Fire-hour (巳/午 hour) Jeongmyo → "Burnout Blueprint"</h3>
<p>If Minseo's hour is 午, she is the highest-risk combination of the year. The June peak will feel euphoric — that's the warning sign, not the reward. Pre-book a week off June 15–22.</p>

<h3>3. Metal-hour (申/酉 hour) Jeongmyo → "Quiet Earner"</h3>
<p>Minseo with a 酉 hour has built-in discipline. Her 2026 window is Aug–Oct; she should stack three invoices into that quarter rather than spreading them.</p>

<h3>4. Water-hour (亥/子 hour) Jeongmyo → "Silent Strategist"</h3>
<p>A 子 hour gives Minseo the cooling her chart needs. She'll feel "slow" all spring. That's correct — her money month is September, not March.</p>

<h2>The Contrarian Take</h2>
<p>The common claim is that 丁卯 benefits from a fire year because fire supports fire. In practice, Jeongmyo is <em>weakened</em> by 丙午 — because adding a bonfire to a lamp does not make a bigger lamp, it makes the lamp irrelevant. That's why the 2026 strategy for Jeongmyo is containment, not expansion.</p>

<h2>When This Doesn't Work</h2>
<ul>
<li><strong>Strong Metal-heavy natal chart:</strong> If your month pillar is 申 or 酉, your chart is already cooled and the "contain fire" rule flips — you can actually push harder in June.</li>
<li><strong>Multiple 卯 in the natal chart:</strong> Two or more 卯 makes the "double seal" problem severe enough that even March is too loud. Move the "ask" window to September instead.</li>
<li><strong>Heavy Saturn transit in Western chart overlay:</strong> If you're in a Saturn return alongside this year, ignore the expansion months entirely. The Saturn clock overrides the Saju clock.</li>
</ul>
