<p>Saju — the Korean system of Four Pillars astrology — is one of the most precise birth chart systems ever developed. <p>The depth is extraordinary. The barrier to entry has always been the same: it requires years of study to interpret with real accuracy, and skilled practitioners have historically been accessible mainly to Korean, Chinese, and diaspora communities where this tradition lives.</p>

<p><strong>Saju AI is dismantling that barrier in 2026.</strong> AI-powered Saju tools are now doing something remarkable: not just calculating your pillars, but interpreting the interactions between them — which elements are in conflict, which are in harmony, which ten-year luck cycle you're currently in, and what that means for career, relationships, and wellbeing in concrete terms.</p>

<p>What does this look like in practice? You enter your birth date and time. The AI calculates your Four Pillars, identifies your Day Master element (the core of your personality and life path in Saju), maps your current luck cycle, and interprets which life areas are being activated or challenged specifically in 2026.</p>

<p>For <strong>career</strong>: if your Day Master is Yang Wood and your current luck cycle introduces strong Metal energy, you're in a classic conflict period — growth meeting resistance. Saju AI doesn't just name the pattern; it explains what that tension typically looks like in practical terms and what kind of action tends to resolve it rather than amplify it.</p>

<p>For <strong>relationships</strong>: if your Saju shows a specific stem combination that creates an elemental clash with a partner's Day Branch, that's not a condemnation — it's information. <p>This level of Saju interpretation was previously available only to those who either studied for years or had access to an expert reader. That access equation is changing fast.</p>
