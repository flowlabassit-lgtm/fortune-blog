<h2>Optimal System Combinations by Decision Type</h2>

<h3>Job Change / Career Transition</h3>

<p><strong>Priority: Saju → Zi Wei Dou Shu → Astrology</strong></p>

<p>When Saju's major cycle flows into Expression (食傷) or Wealth (財星) phases, new challenges are favored. Check which year the Career Palace (官祿宮) in Zi Wei Dou Shu receives auspicious stars. In astrology, <strong>Jupiter transiting the 2nd house (wealth) or 10th house (career)</strong> marks the optimal period.</p>

<p><strong>Periods to avoid:</strong></p>
<ul>
<li>Mercury retrograde — easy to miss key contract terms</li>
<li>Saju Peer/Rob Wealth cycle — increased competition and conflict</li>
<li>Personal Year 9 (Numerology) — a year of completion, not new beginnings</li>
</ul>

<h3>Marriage / Moving In Together</h3>

<p><strong>Priority: Saju compatibility → Tarot → Astrology</strong></p>

<p>In Saju, first look at the Day Branch (日支) harmony. <strong>Six-harmony relationships</strong> like Rat-Ox (子丑合) or Tiger-Pig (寅亥合) indicate solid base compatibility. The Lovers or The World appearing in the outcome position in Tarot is a positive sign.</p>

<p>In astrology, a <strong>Venus-Jupiter trine</strong> forming in a given month is the best timing for marriage. Conversely, marrying during Venus retrograde? Western astrology research statistically associates this with higher divorce rates.</p>

<h3>Investment / Business</h3>

<p><strong>Priority: Zi Wei Dou Shu → Saju → Numerology</strong></p>

<p>When <strong>Wu Qu (武曲星) or Sun (太陽星)</strong> enters the Wealth Palace (財帛宮) in Zi Wei Dou Shu, wealth luck rises. If Saju's Direct Wealth (正財) and Indirect Wealth (偏財) major cycles overlap, the effect is even stronger.</p>

<p>In Numerology, <strong>Personal Year 8</strong> is the year of financial achievement — optimal for investment and business expansion. Personal Year 7, by contrast, is for inner reflection; major financial decisions are best avoided.</p>

<h3>Moving / Emigrating</h3>

<p><strong>Priority: Astrology → Saju → Zi Wei Dou Shu</strong></p>

<p>The transits of the 4th house (home) and 9th house (overseas) in astrology are central. <strong>The 12 months Jupiter transits the 4th house</strong> are the most favorable for moving. In Saju, years when the Traveling Star (驛馬殺) activates carry strong movement energy.</p>

<p>Before a major decision, work through these 5 steps in order:</p>

<p><strong>Step 1: Saju — Where is your major cycle right now?</strong></p>
<p>Check your 10-year major cycle and this year's annual cycle. Expression/Wealth = expansion phase. Peer/Resource = preparation phase. Authority = transition phase.</p>

<p><strong>Step 2: Astrology — Are any planets in retrograde?</strong></p>
<p>Mercury retrograde (watch contracts), Venus retrograde (watch relationships), Mars retrograde (delay action). If in retrograde, wait at least 2 weeks.</p>

<p><strong>Step 3: Tarot — What is the current energy?</strong></p>
<p>Draw a 3-card spread (past · present · future). If <strong>The Hanged Man</strong> or <strong>The Hermit</strong> appears in the present position, that's a "wait" signal.</p>

<p><strong>Step 4: Numerology — What is your Personal Year?</strong></p>
<p>Add your birth date digits to the current year. 1 = new beginning, 4 = building foundation, 8 = achievement, 9 = completion. Start new things in Personal Year 1 or 5.</p>

<p><strong>Step 5: Zi Wei Dou Shu — What stars are in the relevant palace?</strong></p>
<p>Check whether auspicious stars are present in the palace matching your decision type (career = Career Palace, wealth = Wealth Palace, relationships = Spouse Palace).</p>

<h2>3 Common Mistakes</h2>

<h3>Mistake 1: Deciding Based on Only One System</h3>
<p>Don't act immediately just because a good card appeared in Tarot. Tarot only reads current energy. If your Saju major cycle isn't aligned, what looks good short-term can create problems long-term.</p>

<h3>Mistake 2: Treating "Bad Periods" as Absolute Prohibitions</h3>
<p>Stopping everything during Mercury retrograde is an overreaction. Retrograde means <strong>"don't start new things"</strong> — not "do nothing." Things already in progress can continue.</p>

<h3>Mistake 3: Fitting the Interpretation to the Desired Outcome</h3>
<p>Already have a decision you want to make, but the systems say "not yet"? You'll be tempted to ignore that reading or hunt for a different interpretation. <strong>The value of 5-system analysis lies in hearing the answer you don't want to hear.</strong></p>
