<h2>Why Gapja burns out faster than other Wood pillars</h2>
<p>The standard reading says Gapja is "ambitious, pioneering, lucky." That's the gift-shop version. The structural reality: 甲 (Yang Wood) sits directly on 子 (Yang Water), which feeds it constantly. Constant feeding sounds great until you notice the chart has no Fire to <em>use</em> the growth.</p>
<p><strong>The common claim is that Gapja people are natural leaders. In practice, most Gapja charts I've reviewed lead in short bursts and then collapse — because Water without Fire produces ideas without execution.</strong></p>

<h3>Where I got this wrong initially</h3>
<p>My first reading of Gapja charts always weighted the Water positively. More印星 (resource), more support, right? Wrong. When the day master is already 신약 (weak Wood) and Water keeps pouring in, the wood rots before it grows. The fix: stop reading 子 as "support" and start reading it as "pressure to find Fire elsewhere in the chart."</p>

<h2>Four hour pillars, four different Gapja people</h2>
<p>This is what most Gapja write-ups skip. The hour pillar shifts everything.</p>
<ul>
<li><strong>Gapja + 甲子 hour (00-01):</strong> Double Wood-on-Water. Visionary, chronic insomnia, terrible at money. Needs a Fire-element partner.</li>
<li><strong>Gapja + 丙寅 hour (03-05):</strong> Fire shows up. This is the executor variant — Gapja that actually finishes things. Highest entrepreneurial baseline.</li>
<li><strong>Gapja + 戊辰 hour (07-09):</strong> Earth anchors the Wood. Steady careers, slow wealth, often in education or food. Boring on paper, longest-lasting in life.</li>
<li><strong>Gapja + 庚午 hour (11-13):</strong> Metal + Fire combo. Sharp, decisive, often divisive. Excellent for litigators, surgeons, founders. Rough on close relationships.</li>
</ul>
